combines all six core features</strong> requested: professional networking, certification tracking, location-based course finding, continuing education, verified medical reference with citations, and peer collaboration. Healthcare professionals must currently use 5-8 separate tools.</p><h3>Gap 2: Nurses Are Underserved in Professional Networking</h3><p>Doximity and Sermo — the two strongest professional networking platforms — are physician-centric. Doximity includes NPs and PAs but excludes RNs and LPNs. Sermo's APP Community serves NPs and PAs only. There is no "LinkedIn for nurses" that serves all nursing levels (CNA, LPN, RN, BSN, NP, DNP) with robust networking features.</p><h3>Gap 3: Location-Based Recertification Course Finding Does Not Exist as a Feature</h3><p>No platform currently offers an integrated, location-based search for hands-on recertification courses (BLS, ACLS, PALS). Nurses must manually search the AHA Course Catalog, Red Cross website, or Google to find local classes. This is a completely unaddressed need in the current market.</p><h3>Gap 4: Verified Medical Content with Inline Citations Is Paywalled or Absent</h3><p>UpToDate provides excellent evidence-based content with citations but requires expensive institutional subscriptions ($500+/year for individuals). Medscape offers free content but lacks consistent inline citations to peer-reviewed sources. No platform offers nurse-focused, evidence-based clinical reference content with clickable citations to original research at an accessible price point.</p><h3>Gap 5: Certification Tracking Is Disconnected from Education and Networking</h3><p>CerTracker and CE Broker excel at tracking certifications and CE credits, but they exist in isolation. A nurse who discovers an expiring BLS certification in CerTracker must then leave the app, search for a class elsewhere, register on a different website, and manually update their records. There is no seamless workflow from "alert → find course → register → complete → update."</p><h3>Gap 6: Peer Collaboration for Clinical Learning Is Fragmented</h3><p>Sermo offers clinical Q&amp;A for NPs/PAs, but there is no equivalent for RNs. Nurses resort to Facebook groups, Reddit (r/nursing), and informal channels for peer clinical discussions — environments that are unverified, unmoderated for clinical accuracy, and not HIPAA-compliant.</p><h3>Gap 7: No Platform Bridges the Education-to-Practice Gap</h3><p>Current platforms either focus on formal CE (Nurse.com, CE Broker) or clinical reference (Medscape, UpToDate), but none bridge the gap between continuing education and point-of-care clinical reference. A platform that connects learning modules to clinical reference content would create a unique value proposition.</p><hr><h2>1.5 Market Opportunity Summary</h2><p>The total addressable market includes approximately:</p><ul> <li><strong>4.7 million</strong> registered nurses (RNs) in the United States</li> <li><strong>355,000+</strong> nurse practitioners (NPs)</li> <li><strong>1.0 million+</strong> licensed practical nurses (LPNs/LVNs)</li> <li><strong>1.5 million+</strong> certified nursing assistants (CNAs)</li> <li><strong>Additional:</strong> PAs, respiratory therapists, paramedics, and other allied health professionals</li> </ul><p>All of these professionals need certification tracking, continuing education, and clinical reference tools. information published on the platform must adhere to the following sourcing hierarchy:</p><ol> <li><strong>Tier 1 — Highest Authority:</strong> Systematic reviews and meta-analyses from Cochrane Library, peer-reviewed journals (NEJM, JAMA, Lancet, Annals of Internal Medicine, etc.)</li> <li><strong>Tier 2 — Strong Evidence:</strong> Randomized controlled trials (RCTs) published in peer-reviewed journals indexed in PubMed/MEDLINE</li> <li><strong>Tier 3 — Established Guidelines:</strong> Clinical practice guidelines from recognized bodies (AHA, ACC, IDSA, CDC, WHO, ACOG, etc.)</li> <li><strong>Tier 4 — Authoritative Textbooks:</strong> Content from accredited medical and nursing textbooks (e.g., Brunner &amp; Suddarth's Textbook of Medical-Surgical Nursing, Pharmacology for Nursing Care by Lehne, etc.)</li> <li><strong>Tier 5 — Expert Consensus:</strong> Expert opinion and consensus statements from professional organizations (used only when higher-level evidence is unavailable, clearly labeled as such)</li> </ol><p><strong>Content that does NOT meet these standards will not be published.</strong> Blog posts, opinion pieces, and non-peer-reviewed sources are excluded from the medical reference library (though they may appear in the news feed with appropriate labeling).</p><h3>2.3.2 Content Verification Process</h3><table class="e-rte-table">
